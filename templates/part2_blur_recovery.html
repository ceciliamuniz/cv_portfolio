How It Works
1. Apply Gaussian blur: L_b = L ⊛ G
2. FFT conversion: FFT(L_b) = FFT(L) × FFT(G)
3. Wiener deconvolution: L = IFFT(FFT(L_b) ÷ (FFT(G) + noise))

Technical Implementation

Gaussian Kernel

2D Gaussian function creates smooth blur effect

FFT Transform

Converts convolution to multiplication in frequency domain

Deconvolution

Division in frequency domain reverses blur operation

Wiener Filter

Regularization prevents noise amplification

Limitations of Deblurring
  • High-frequency information is permanently lost during blurring
  • Noise gets amplified during deconvolution process
  • Perfect recovery is theoretically impossible
  • Wiener filtering provides better stability than direct division
